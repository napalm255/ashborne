# Ashborne — Game World Zones & Areas
### Location Design Document

---

## Overview

The game world is organized into **Zones** — distinct geographic areas based on real locations in and around Frederick County, Maryland. Each Zone is an explorable overworld where Maddox and Dexter move freely, encounter NPCs, discover clues, and trigger scene transitions into interior locations (buildings, basements, tunnels).

**Fast Travel** is unlocked per zone as the story progresses. Once a zone is "activated" through story events, Maddox can pull up the regional map on his phone and fast travel to any previously visited zone. Travel time is simulated (not real-time) but reflected in dialogue and ambient detail — arriving at Hagerstown at night feels different than arriving at noon.

## Zone 1 — Downtown Frederick
**Designation:** HUB / Home Base
**Unlock:** Game start

The core of the game world. Frederick's historic downtown is a dense, walkable zone packed with shops, alleys, restaurants, and civic buildings. Carroll Creek runs through the center. The streets here are cobblestone-era brick under modern pavement, and the buildings remember things.

Maddox's apartment and workshop are located here — a second-floor space above a print shop on a side street off Market Street, with a private entrance and a rear alley Dexter uses as a yard.

### Key Locations (Exterior → Interior)

**Maddox's Apartment & Workshop**
The home base. Workbench, monitors, gear storage, Dexter's bed (which is larger than Maddox's). Fast travel always available from here. Serves as the inventory, crafting, and save hub.

**Frederick City Hall / IT Infrastructure Room**
Where the inciting contract takes place. The public-facing city offices are accessible; the server room in the basement requires either a keycard (obtained via story) or Maddox bypassing the access panel. The anomalous partition is discovered here.

**Colt & Compass Antiquities — Patrick Street**
Vivienne Colt's shop. Exterior is a narrow brick storefront with a green awning. Interior is a labyrinth of shelves, display cases, and back rooms. Vivienne's private archive is behind a locked door in the back — unlocked mid-Act I. Critical resource hub throughout the game.

**Mercer's Auto & Cycle**
Sal's shop on the south end of downtown. Garage bay, parts room, a back office Sal uses for everything including eating lunch and occasionally sleeping. This is where Maddox builds devices, stashes gear, and decompresses. One of the few places Dexter is completely relaxed.

**Frederick County Historical Society**
A formal research institution on West Patrick Street. Public reading room accessible freely; restricted archive requires either a library card (obtainable) or charm/deception with the archivist. Contains Ironveil-adjacent documents the player needs in Act II.

**The Monocacy Pub**
A downtown bar Maddox frequents. Good place to gather rumors, overhear conversations, or meet contacts. The bartender knows more than he lets on. Dexter is allowed on the patio.

**Frederick Memorial Hospital — Emergency Department**
Nora's workplace. Accessible during story beats. Not a combat zone — a place for information, cutscenes, and checking in with Nora. Parking garage behind it becomes relevant in Act II.

**An Alley Off East All Saints Street**
No sign. No name. Just a door with a buzzer. Someone Maddox knows from the gray-market side of his work operates here. Useful for gear and information that can't be obtained through official channels.

## Zone 2 — Monocacy National Battlefield
**Designation:** Story Zone / Outdoor Exploration
**Unlock:** End of Act I
**Distance from Frederick:** ~4 miles south via MD-355
**Ride Time:** ~8 minutes

The Monocacy battlefield sits southeast of Frederick along the Monocacy River, preserved as a National Park. In daylight it's quiet — rolling farmland, interpretive signs, the occasional school group. At night, it's something else. The battle of July 9, 1864 killed and wounded nearly 2,000 men here in a single day, and the ground has never fully settled.

This is where the Ashborne fed. The entity's presence is stronger here than anywhere else in the game — environmental effects are more pronounced, Dexter's reactions more intense, and certain areas of the battlefield are inaccessible until Maddox has the right tools or story context.

### Key Locations

**The Visitor Center**
Publicly accessible. Contains historical exhibits that serve as lore delivery — maps of the battle, accounts of what soldiers reported seeing, a specific photograph in the collection that stops Maddox cold.

**The Best Farm**
A historic farmhouse on the battlefield grounds. The ground floor is a public exhibit. The cellar is not. Getting into the cellar is a puzzle — it requires tools Maddox has to build, and what's down there is the first physical evidence of the Ironveil.

**The River Bank — Monocacy Ford**
Where the fighting was heaviest. At night, with the right equipment, Maddox can detect RF anomalies in the area consistent with the signal buried in Frederick's network. The river here is shallow enough to cross on foot. The other side is not safe.

**The Cornfield**
A large open area of the battlefield that functions as the zone's most dangerous outdoor area in the late game. Avoid at night until equipped. Dexter refuses to enter without Maddox initiating a specific command sequence.

## Zone 3 — Walkersville
**Designation:** Secondary Town / NPC Hub
**Unlock:** Act I, after first visit to Historical Society
**Distance from Frederick:** ~7 miles northeast via MD-194
**Ride Time:** ~13 minutes

A small, quiet town that feels exactly like what it is — a place where people have lived for generations and intend to keep doing so. Walkersville has the specific energy of somewhere that minds its business while knowing everyone else's. It also, as Maddox discovers, has a higher-than-average concentration of Ironveil descendants who never left.

### Key Locations

**Walkersville Community Library**
Small but thorough local history section. The librarian has worked here for 31 years and has a memory like a filing cabinet. She is not an Ironveil member but she has pieced together more of the truth than anyone has told her.

**The Harmon House**
A residential property that belonged to one of the original Ironveil members. Currently owned by an elderly woman named Edna Harmon who is absolutely not going to tell Maddox anything without significant effort. Her attic is the goal. Getting to the attic is a multi-step puzzle involving trust, a distraction, and Dexter doing something useful.

**Walkersville Fire Station**
One of Maddox's older contacts works here. Not story-critical but a useful source of county infrastructure information — access roads, utility tunnels, flood control systems under Frederick that don't appear on public maps.

**The Grain Mill (Abandoned)**
East of town, off the main road. Sits on a property that shows up in Ironveil documents as a secondary meeting location. Currently condemned. Currently not empty.

## Zone 4 — Libertytown
**Designation:** Isolated Zone / Puzzle Focus
**Unlock:** Act II
**Distance from Frederick:** ~12 miles east via MD-26
**Ride Time:** ~19 minutes

Libertytown is less a town and more an idea of one — a small rural crossroads community that time has moved gently past. The drive out on MD-26 takes you through farmland and tree cover that thickens as you go, and Libertytown itself feels quieter than it should for its proximity to Frederick. The Ashborne's cipher includes a coordinate that points here. Maddox isn't sure what he's going to find. He's less sure after he gets there.

### Key Locations

**St. Paul's Lutheran Church**
One of the oldest standing structures in the area. The church is active but small. The graveyard behind it contains markers that match names from the Ironveil documents — including one stone that has no name, only a symbol. The symbol matches the cipher.

**The Crossroads**
The literal intersection at the center of Libertytown. Folklore in the region (Vivienne knows it, the Walkersville librarian alluded to it) holds that the crossroads here sits on a ley line intersection. In the game, this manifests as a location where Maddox's electronic equipment behaves strangely and Dexter sits down and will not move until Maddox completes a specific action.

**Abandoned Farmstead — Route 26 East**
A property that appears in county records as having changed hands seventeen times since 1870, always under unusual circumstances. The farmhouse is structurally questionable. The root cellar beneath it is not on any record. This is where the Ironveil's primary sealing mechanism was constructed. Pieces of it are still there.

## Zone 5 — Middletown
**Designation:** Secondary Town / Mid-Act II Hub
**Unlock:** Act II
**Distance from Frederick:** ~8 miles northwest via US-40 Alt
**Ride Time:** ~13 minutes

Middletown sits in the Middletown Valley between Catoctin Mountain and South Mountain — a genuinely beautiful stretch of western Frederick County that feels geographically cut off from Frederick proper. The mountains on either side give the town a hemmed-in quality that the story leans into. Something came through South Mountain during the Civil War. The accounts were dismissed as battlefield hysteria. They weren't.

### Key Locations

**The South Mountain Inn**
A historic inn and restaurant on Alternate US-40 at the crest of South Mountain. One of the Keeper's known associates meets contacts here. Nice enough that Maddox is slightly out of place. Dexter waits outside, which several diners photograph.

**Middletown Valley Historical Museum**
Small local museum with a specific collection of South Mountain battle artifacts. One exhibit — a collection of recovered personal effects from Confederate soldiers — contains an item that shouldn't exist. The curator doesn't know what it is. Maddox does.

**The Old Lutheran Church — Main Street**
A still-active church with a congregation that has maintained the same families for over a century. The pastor is a seventh-generation Middletown resident. He knows what Maddox is looking for. He's been expecting someone to come asking. He's not sure if he's relieved or terrified that it's a guy with a motorcycle and a pitbull.

**South Mountain State Battlefield**
Accessible from Middletown via Alt US-40. An outdoor area — wooded, hilly, physically demanding to traverse. A tunnel system beneath the mountain connects to the Frederick underground network in the game's fiction. The entrance is hidden. Dexter finds it.

## Zone 6 — Hagerstown
**Designation:** Major City / Late-Game Zone
**Unlock:** Late Act II
**Distance from Frederick:** ~25 miles northwest via I-70
**Ride Time:** ~34 minutes

The farthest zone from Frederick and the biggest. Hagerstown is a real city with real density — more urban than anything else in the game, with a downtown that has seen better decades but retains the bones of something that mattered. The Keeper has a presence here that they don't have in the smaller towns; this is where resources, logistics, and personnel have been quietly assembled. Hagerstown is the Keeper's operational base, as opposed to Frederick, which is the Keeper's target.

The longer ride on I-70 is reflected in the game — fast travel here has a longer loading/transition sequence with Maddox and Dexter on the highway, which the first time through contains visual anomalies in the rearview mirror.

### Key Locations

**The Maryland Theatre — South Potomac Street**
A historic theater that serves as a meeting point and, in the late game, a confrontation location. The backstage and basement of the theater have been modified. The modifications are not for theatrical purposes.

**Antique Row — Jonathan Street**
A strip of antique and second-hand shops where the Keeper has been quietly acquiring specific artifacts for years. The dealers don't know who their buyer is. Matching the acquisition records to the Ironveil artifact list is an Act II puzzle chain.

**The Washington County Free Library**
Larger research facility than anything in Frederick. Contains newspaper archives going back to the Civil War that Maddox needs — specifically, coverage of an incident in 1864 that was reported once and never followed up on.

**The Warehouse District**
Industrial area on the south end of Hagerstown. One of the warehouses is registered to a shell company. The shell company connects to the Keeper. What's inside is the clearest evidence yet that the Ashborne's release isn't accidental — it's been planned, and the planning has been going on for a very long time.

**Hagerstown Regional Airport (HGR)**
Small regional airport. Relevant in the late game when it becomes clear that the Keeper has an exit strategy. Stopping that strategy is a timed sequence.

### Fast Travel Rules (Game Mechanics)

- **Unlock condition:** Each zone must be visited via motorcycle at least once before fast travel is available.
- **Time of day matters:** Some locations are only accessible or only dangerous at specific times. Fast travel prompts Maddox to select arrival time.
- **Zone state persists:** NPCs remember what you've done. Shops restock. Enemies respawn on a cycle. Some areas permanently change state after story events.
- **Dexter indicator:** On the fast travel map, Dexter's posture icon reflects the threat level of the destination zone at the current story point. Relaxed = safe. Alert = active threats. Hackles up = something wrong.
- **Hagerstown penalty:** First fast travel to Hagerstown triggers a brief unskippable cutscene (the highway anomaly). Subsequent fast travels are standard.
