# Ashborne: World Overview
## Frederick, Maryland — 2025

---

## Setting

**Frederick, Maryland, October 2025.** A city of 80,000+ residents, midway between Washington DC and Baltimore, positioned at the heart of the American mid-Atlantic. Historic brick storefronts line Market Street. Carroll Creek runs through downtown, recently revitalized as a waterfront district. Craft breweries, coffee shops, and galleries occupy the second floors of 19th-century buildings. Tourism boards market Frederick as "charming," "walkable," "picturesque."

Beneath that postcard aesthetic lies the weight of actual history—not sanitized, not forgotten. Frederick was occupied by both Union and Confederate forces during the Civil War. The Battle of Monocacy, fought four miles south of the city center, killed and wounded nearly 2,000 men in a single day (July 9, 1864). The ground has never fully settled.

And beneath *that*—in the stone foundations of buildings, in the tunnels under the historic district, in the digital infrastructure of a city mid-migration to the cloud—something has been waiting for 161 years.

---

## 2025: The Contrast

Frederick in 2025 is a study in contradiction. The city has:

- **Tech presence:** Proximity to Fort Meade and NSA means significant cybersecurity industry presence; several mid-size tech firms have offices downtown. Gigabit fiber infrastructure.
- **Digital infrastructure:** Recent Azure migration (2023) for city services, Cisco Meraki cloud-managed networks, Verkada camera systems, modern smart-city telemetry.
- **Historic preservation:** Rigorous preservation boards, walking tours, Civil War reenactments on summer weekends.

The power of the story lives in this collision: hyper-connected, 2025-infrastructure containing something from 1864. The Ashborne is not modern. It does not think in modern terms. But it has learned to speak in digital signals, to propagate through fiber and wireless, to adapt. The ancient and the contemporary have collided.

Maddox Grey, a freelance penetration tester with a Flipper Zero and a custom Kali Linux laptop, stumbles into this collision on a Tuesday when he's trying to find budget vulnerabilities. He finds something far older than budget vulnerabilities instead.

## Geography: The Zones

The game world encompasses six distinct zones, all based on real locations:

1. **Downtown Frederick** (home base) — The walkable core, Carroll Creek, historic district
2. **Monocacy National Battlefield** (4 miles south) — Where the battle happened, where the Ashborne fed
3. **Walkersville** (7 miles northeast) — Small rural town, concentration of Ironveil descendants
4. **Libertytown** (12 miles east) — Isolated crossroads, holds coordinates to the sealing sites
5. **Middletown** (8 miles northwest) — Mountain valley, South Mountain tunnel network
6. **Hagerstown** (25 miles northwest) — Largest city in the region, the Keeper's operational base

Fast travel between zones is motorcycle-based, simulated but reflected in dialogue and ambient detail. Arriving at Hagerstown at midnight feels different than arriving at noon. Weather states, time of day, and phenomenon intensity all affect the experience of traveling.

---

## Frederick's Digital Backbone

The city's infrastructure is split between legacy on-premises systems and modern cloud services:

- **City IT:** Hybrid Azure + Active Directory, cloud migration completed in 2023
- **Security cameras:** Cisco Meraki network, cloud-managed
- **Body cameras:** Recently moved to Axon system with Verkada cloud storage (public-facing employees: police, fire, public works)
- **Infrastructure telemetry:** Smart city sensors on water authority, traffic control, maintenance monitoring
- **Utilities:** One legacy server from the 2004 build that was never decommissioned because no one could figure out what it did

The anomalous partition lives on that last server. It has propagated through the city network using RF signals, maintenance node wireless, and MQTT protocols. It is older than electricity and more modern than current TCP/IP stacks. It is waiting.

## Atmosphere & Tone

**Gabriel Knight meets Diablo.**

- **Literary, atmospheric:** The writing is adult, grounded, occasionally funny in the way that exhausted people are funny. Rich environmental detail and characterization.
- **Isometric presentation:** 3D world viewed at a ~45-degree angle (like Baldur's Gate 3, Hades). Camera is semi-fixed, allowing pan/zoom. Nanite geometry provides tactile detail—the brick is weathered, the server rack has dust, the old wood holds splinters.
- **Lighting is purposeful:** Shadows are dramatic. Cold zones are literally blue-tinted. The Ashborne's influence is visible as light sources failing, displays corrupting, heat signatures inverting.
- **Investigation-first:** Action is rare. Tension comes from discovery, from systems failing, from the quiet moments when Dexter goes still and you know something is wrong.
- **Grounded horror:** The supernatural is treated as a technical problem Maddox doesn't yet have the framework to solve. It's scary because it's real and it's breaking the rules of systems Maddox understands.

## Key Thematic Threads

**The past doesn't stay buried—it encrypts itself and waits.**
Ironveil knowledge has been preserved through generations, through documents and symbols, through maintained seals. The Ashborne has also been waiting, not dying, not weakening, just dormant. When it wakes up, both sides of this 161-year standoff are ready. What wasn't ready is Maddox.

**Technology as a double-edged instrument.**
Maddox's greatest asset is also what the Ashborne uses against him. His RF analyzer detects the entity, but also attracts its attention. His laptop can decrypt the cipher, but also becomes a vector for influence. Every tool is a vulnerability.

**Loyalty vs. isolation.**
Maddox's instinct is to go it alone. Dexter is the one constant. Wren forces him to share what he knows. Nora sees the toll. Sal provides logistics. None of this would work if Maddox stayed isolated, but isolation is his default.

**Frederick as a character.**
The city is beautiful and corrupted, historic and hyper-connected, innocent and haunted. It has a personality. It has memory. What happens to it matters.

## Calendar & Timing

The game takes place across approximately 18 days spread across three acts:

- **Act I:** October 7–19, 2025 (13 days)
- **Act II:** October 20–26, 2025 (7 days, approx.)
- **Act III:** October 27–28, 2025 (climax, intensive)

Fast travel can simulate weeks passing in moments, but the main story progresses in late October, autumn in Frederick. The weather is crisp. The trees are turning. The nights are getting longer. It's a good time for things to go wrong.
