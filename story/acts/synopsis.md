# Ashborne: A Maddox Grey Mystery
## Synopsis — Frederick, Maryland, 2025

---

## Logline

A freelance white-hat hacker stumbles onto an encrypted Civil War-era signal buried in Frederick's municipal network — and accidentally wakes up whatever has been waiting inside it for 161 years.

---

## Setting

Frederick, Maryland. A city that looks like a charming mid-Atlantic postcard — brick storefronts, farmers markets, craft breweries, historic preservation district — and underneath all of it, a place that bled for four years during the Civil War and never quite healed right. The old buildings downtown remember things. So do the tunnels beneath them.

**Year**: 2025. Frederick's infrastructure is mid-migration to the cloud (Microsoft Azure completed 2023), but legacy on-premise servers from the 2004 build still run critical systems. The city is hyper-connected, smart-city sensors everywhere, security cameras on Cisco Meraki networks. Modern technology containing something from 1864.

---

## Overview

Maddox Grey doesn't go looking for trouble. Trouble finds him on a Tuesday in April 2025, buried inside a routine penetration test for Frederick's municipal IT infrastructure. What looks like legacy code turns out to be something far older than any codebase has a right to be — a self-replicating cipher that predates modern computing, somehow encoded into the city's digital infrastructure like a parasite that's been dormant, waiting for the right person to poke it.

Within 48 hours, a city engineer is dead. The official story is a heart attack. The security footage from City Hall is corrupted in a way Maddox has never seen — not deleted, not looped. *Rewritten.* As if something reached into the file and changed what happened.

Maddox, with his pitbull Dexter riding shotgun in the sidecar of his motorcycle, starts pulling threads. What he finds beneath Frederick's surface — literally and figuratively — is a demonic entity known in fragmented Civil War-era documents as **the Ashborne**, a presence that fed on the carnage of the Battle of Monocacy in 1864 and was intentionally sealed beneath the city by a secret order of soldiers, clergymen, and one very unlikely telegraph operator named Clara Marsh.

The seal is cracking. Someone in Frederick's present is helping it crack. And Maddox Grey, whether he likes it or not, is now the only person who knows enough to stop it.

He is not alone. Wren Calloway, a historic preservation architect and cultural resources manager for the Monocacy National Battlefield, has been documenting the same phenomenon from the physical side for three months and is looking for answers in the same places Maddox is searching. Their investigation becomes partnership. The partnership becomes something more.

---

## Act Structure

### Act I — The Intrusion
**Timeframe**: April 7–19, 2025 (two weeks)

Maddox takes a routine city infrastructure contract. While running a penetration test on Frederick's municipal network, he discovers an anomalous encrypted partition no one can explain. The city IT director dismisses it as old junk data. Two days later, the IT director is dead. Maddox starts digging. Dexter starts growling at things that aren't there.

He meets Wren Calloway at the Monocacy Battlefield, where she's been logging RF anomalies that correlate exactly with his findings. Their independent investigations cross-reference. She has the historical knowledge. He has the technical tools. Neither can solve it alone.

By the end of Act I, Maddox has committed to staying in the investigation. The partition is not a network error. Something sealed itself in Frederick's digital infrastructure 161 years ago, and it's waking up.

**Key locations**: Downtown Frederick, City Hall, Monocacy Battlefield, Colt & Compass Antiquities, Mercer's Auto & Cycle, Historical Society

**Puzzles**: Network intrusion, signal analysis, document decryption, rapport building, lock bypassing

---

### Act II — The History
**Timeframe**: May–June 2025 (2–3 weeks)

The cipher leads to the Battle of Monocacy (July 9, 1864) and a secret order called **the Ironveil** — soldiers, clergymen, and a telegraph operator who witnessed something on the battlefield that wasn't human and spent their remaining lives ensuring it stayed buried. Their descendants still live in Frederick. Some know exactly what Maddox found. Not all of them want him to survive knowing it.

Wren and Maddox navigate Walkersville, Libertytown, and Middletown, building relationships with Ironveil descendants who control pieces of the historical record. Edna Harmon, Reverend Carl Weiss, and Reverend James Tillman each guard knowledge that, combined, reveals what the Ashborne is and how it was sealed.

The Keeper — someone deliberately dismantling the seal to release the Ashborne — becomes visible. Their identity is revealed mid-Act II. Their motivation is complicated: they believe the Ashborne can be controlled. They are wrong.

Wren and Maddox become lovers. The stakes become personal.

**Key locations**: Monocacy Battlefield (Best Farm), Walkersville (Harmon House), Libertytown (St. Paul's Church, Farmstead), Middletown (Old Lutheran Church, South Mountain tunnel), Hagerstown (Maryland Theatre, Warehouse District)

**Puzzles**: Reconstructing historical documents, decoding Ironveil symbols, underground navigation prep, confronting the Keeper

---

### Act III — The Fracture
**Timeframe**: Late June 2025 (final days, intensive)

The Ashborne begins directly influencing people — small at first, then catastrophically. Maddox realizes the entity has been watching him since he found the partition. The final confrontation requires everything: all of his Tier 3 gear, all of the Ironveil's accumulated knowledge, and Wren's structural expertise navigating Frederick's sealed tunnel network.

The sealing ritual — performed 161 years ago to contain the Ashborne — must be executed again. But this time, using the complete cipher (fragments of which Maddox has collected across the entire investigation) and at all three original sealing points simultaneously.

The resolution is not a victory in the traditional sense. The Ashborne cannot be destroyed. It must be contained. The seal must hold.

**Key locations**: City Hall server room, Colt & Compass cellar, underground tunnel network, Libertytown farmstead, Best Farm, Middletown undercroft, warehouse confrontation

**Puzzles**: Assembling Tier 3 gear, reconstructing the complete cipher, navigating the underground, executing the sealing ritual, final confrontation with the Keeper and the Ashborne

## Themes

- **The past doesn't stay buried** — it encrypts itself and waits. History is not resolved; it's just dormant until someone wakes it.
- **Technology as a double-edged tool** — Maddox's greatest asset is also what the Ashborne uses against him. Digital infrastructure contains analog horror.
- **Loyalty vs. isolation** — Dexter is the anchor. Wren forces Maddox to trust. The investigation requires partnership, and Maddox's instinct is always to go it alone.
- **Containment, not destruction** — Some things cannot be destroyed. They must be held. The ending is not triumph but maintenance.
- **Frederick as a character** — The city is beautiful, historic, and hiding something. What happens to it matters.

---

## Tone

Gritty, grounded, and occasionally funny in the way that people are funny when they're terrified and too stubborn to admit it. Think Gabriel Knight's atmosphere with a protagonist who responds to a demon manifestation by muttering *"well that's not in the API documentation."*

The isometric camera emphasizes distance and observation — you're looking down at the mystery, seeing the whole scene, but knowing you can't understand it from a distance. You have to descend into it.

Literary and atmospheric. Rich environmental detail. Dialogue is precise and purposeful. The game trusts the player to pick up subtle beats rather than over-explaining.

## Character Relationships

**Maddox Grey**: Protagonist. Freelance white-hat hacker. Brilliant but isolated. Trusts Dexter more than people. Becomes less isolated across the narrative.

**Dexter**: Maddox's pitbull companion. The emotional anchor and the most reliable instrument when electronic systems fail. Immune to the Ashborne's disruption in ways that matter.

**Wren Calloway**: Love interest and co-investigator. Historic preservation architect and NPS cultural resources manager. Her historical knowledge is essential to understanding the Ashborne. Her presence forces Maddox to trust.

**Vivienne Colt**: Antiquarian shopkeeper. Has been waiting for someone to find the Ashborne for years. Provides historical context and Ironveil artifacts.

**Sal Mercer**: Best friend and mechanic. The only person Maddox truly confides in early on. Provides logistics and a connection to normalcy.

**Nora Grey**: Maddox's sister, ER nurse. Notices the toll the investigation takes on him. Becomes increasingly concerned.

**Detective Ray Okafor**: Police detective investigating the IT director's death. Initially an obstacle. Becomes an unlikely ally as he realizes something supernatural is occurring.

**The Keeper**: Primary human antagonist. Deliberately dismantling the seal. Motivation is complex — they believe the Ashborne can be controlled.

**The Ashborne**: The entity itself. Never fully seen, only perceived through its effects. Ancient. Hungry. Patient.
