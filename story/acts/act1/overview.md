# Act 1 — The Intrusion

**Status**: Complete design documentation

**Timeframe**: April 7–19, 2025 (approximately 13 days)

**Premise**: A routine penetration test becomes an investigation into something sealed in Frederick's network for 161 years. Within 48 hours of discovery, the city IT director is dead. The investigation is Maddox's alone until he meets Wren Calloway at the Monocacy Battlefield, where she's been documenting the same phenomenon independently.

---

## Story Arc Summary

Maddox Grey accepts a routine contract to assess Frederick's municipal network infrastructure. He discovers an anomalous encrypted partition on a legacy server. The city IT director dismisses it. The director dies within 48 hours.

The security footage is corrupted in an impossible way. Maddox's RF analyzer picks up signal traces that correlate to the partition's activity. He starts treating it as a genuine investigation.

His search leads to Vivienne Colt's antiquities shop, where historical documents prove the cipher pattern dates to 1864. The Battle of Monocacy. The Ironveil. An entity called the Ashborne.

He meets Wren Calloway at the Monocacy Battlefield. Her RF data (from her own investigation) perfectly correlates with his. They form a working alliance based on practical need and complementary expertise.

By Act I's end, Maddox has committed to staying in the investigation. He understands this isn't a technical problem to be solved and closed. It's a 161-year-old seal that's cracking, and something dangerous is leaking through.

## Key Beats (Numbered)

1. **The Contract** (April 8) — Maddox accepts pen test work for Frederick city infrastructure
2. **City Hall Discovery** (April 9–10) — Anomalous partition found on legacy server; RF analyzer detects impossible signals
3. **Hale's Dismissal** (April 10) — City IT director dismisses the finding as irrelevant
4. **First Wren Encounter** (April 10, evening) — Wren stops Maddox at City Hall basement; she's investigating foundation cracks
5. **Hale's Death** (April 12) — Official cardiac event; security footage mysteriously corrupted
6. **Maddox's Commitment** (April 13) — He decides to investigate despite lacking legal standing
7. **Carroll Creek Recon** (April 14) — RF anomaly detected at creek maintenance node; signal propagates through entire city network
8. **Vivienne Colt's Archive** (April 14) — Civil War documents prove cipher is 161 years old; first mention of Ironveil
9. **Second Wren Encounter** (April 14, night) — Wren finds Maddox at Monocacy Battlefield with RF equipment; Scout and Dexter both alert on same location
10. **Detective Okafor** (April 15) — Police investigation begins; Okafor connects Maddox to Hale's death
11. **Historical Society** (April 17–18) — Archive access reveals Ironveil documents and soldier photographs with suspicious symbols
12. **Vivienne's Deeper Archive** (April 18) — The Ironveil is named; Clara Marsh identified as telegraph operator founder; sealing ritual described
13. **Working Alliance** (April 18, evening) — Wren and Maddox compare data; perfect correlation proves both are investigating the same phenomenon
14. **Hale's Hidden Documentation** (April 19) — Maddox cracks Hale's workstation; encrypted notes reveal 14 months of anomaly logs predating Maddox's involvement
15. **Ghost Access** (April 19, morning) — Someone remotely accesses the partition from an unoccupied terminal; Maddox witnesses it on camera
16. **Wren's Email & Meeting** (April 19, evening) — Wren reaches out; they meet at The Monocacy Pub with their dogs; establish that this is real and dangerous
17. **The Commitment** (April 19, midnight) — Maddox types `ashborne_active.enc` in his field notebook; commits to investigation

## Character Introductions & Development

**Maddox Grey**:
- Starts confident in technical expertise
- Gradually realizes this problem has no technical solution
- Moves from isolation to partnership with Wren
- Commits to something beyond his control

**Dexter**:
- Supernatural sensitivity first evident in Act I
- Becomes primary reliable instrument as investigation deepens
- Phoenix patch unexplained but noted

**Wren Calloway**:
- Introduced as obstacle, becomes investigative partner
- Brings historical knowledge to complement Maddox's technical expertise
- Acts 1 establishes intellectual compatibility; Acts 2–3 deepen emotional connection

**Vivienne Colt**:
- First named ally; provides historical context
- Has been waiting for this; adds weight to Maddox's discovery
- Establishes Ironveil as real historical organization

**Sal Mercer**:
- Established as Maddox's closest friend and practical support
- Non-judgmental, asks questions but doesn't pry
- Provides workshop space and logistics

**Nora Grey**:
- Introduces via text about Hale's death (her workplace)
- Sister relationship established; she notices changes in Maddox
- Personal stakes anchor to Frederick's population

**Detective Okafor**:
- Professional antagonist; investigating from official angle
- Creates tension (Maddox must avoid obstruction of justice charges)
- Respectful but skeptical of Maddox's theories

**Thomas Hale** (deceased):
- Known through investigation and documents
- Establishes death as turning point, not accident
- His hidden logs become investigative resource

## Tone Notes

Act I is grounded realism with cracks showing. No impossible events occur on-screen. All anomalies have possible mundane explanations that Maddox hasn't ruled out. The supernatural is present in Dexter's reactions and the corrupted footage, but hasn't broken through the plausible surface.

By Act I's end, Maddox intellectually understands something impossible is occurring. The player understands it too. The transition from "this is weird" to "this is real" completes Act I.

The atmosphere should feel like a mystery novel's first act — pieces scattered, patterns beginning to emerge, but the full picture not yet visible. Tension builds from curiosity to concern to commitment.
