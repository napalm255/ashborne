# Character Profile — Dexter

---

## Basic Info

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Dexter |
| **Breed** | American Pit Bull Terrier |
| **Age** | 5 |
| **Coat** | Solid black with a white chest patch |
| **Weight** | ~65 lbs |
| **Role** | Constant companion, co-investigator, supernatural sensor |

---

## Appearance

Dexter is a solidly built black pitbull with a short, gleaming coat and a chest that hits you first — because of his size, and because of the white patch. The patch is unmistakable: irregular, bright, and shaped with enough suggestion of wings and a rising form that people who see it tend to do a double-take. Maddox has always called it the Phoenix. Strangers call it unsettling in the best possible way.

His eyes are amber, alert, and almost uncomfortably attentive. He carries himself with the calm confidence of an animal that has never had a reason to be nervous and wouldn't show it if he did.

When he rides in his sidecar on the motorcycle, his head is up, ears back, watching everything. People photograph him at red lights. He tolerates this without reaction.

---

## Personality

Dexter is, by any reasonable measure, an exceptionally good dog. He is calm in crowds, patient with strangers who approach respectfully, and completely unbothered by loud noises, motorcycles, or the general chaos of Maddox's life. He has been trained extensively — commands in both English and hand signal — and follows them with the kind of reliability that makes professional trainers nod slowly and say "huh."

He is also, when the situation calls for it, absolutely not a dog you want between you and a wall.

Dexter doesn't bluff. He doesn't bark to hear himself bark. When he goes still and quiet and his attention locks onto something — a person, a doorway, a direction Maddox can't see anything in — Maddox has learned to stop what he's doing and pay attention. In this story, that instinct proves more valuable than any sensor Maddox could build.

He is loyal with a completeness that isn't really a character trait so much as a fact of nature. Maddox is his person. That's the whole equation.

---

## The Sidecar

Dexter has a custom-fitted sidecar on Maddox's motorcycle. It has a weather cover, a harness anchor point that keeps Dexter secure during aggressive cornering, and a small storage compartment that Dexter has learned holds treats. He rides with his head up and his ears back, watching everything. People stop and photograph him at red lights. He tolerates this.

The sidecar is reinforced to handle Dexter's weight and momentum. It's well-maintained. It is, essentially, Dexter's domain on the bike—he knows how to move within it, how to shift his weight during turns, how to balance.

---

## Role in the Game

Dexter functions as Maddox's sensory extension into the supernatural — before Maddox has any framework to understand what he's dealing with, Dexter already knows something is wrong. His reactions serve as early warning systems, environmental clues, and occasional puzzle solutions (tracking, locating hidden spaces, alerting Maddox to things electronic sensors miss).

He can be directed via commands in gameplay:

- **Search** — Dexter sweeps an area systematically and alerts on anything anomalous (unusual scent, temperature variance, presence he recognizes as dangerous)
- **Guard** — Dexter holds position and warns if anything approaches (bark alert, body stiffening, immediate attention)
- **Track** — Follows a scent trail to a source or location (unlocked mid-game, requires Maddox to have first established that Dexter can be trusted on this)
- **Stand Down** — Dexter disengages from alert mode (sometimes Dexter's read on a person is correct but the situation requires diplomacy or restraint)

In addition to explicit commands, Dexter's baseline behaviors are constant communication:

- Sniffing along the floor = something hidden below or recently passed through
- Sitting and staring at a wall = hollow space, hidden door, or occult inscription behind
- Refusing to enter a zone = active supernatural hazard, requires preparation or protection before entry
- Hackles raised, stationary = immediate threat nearby (human or supernatural)
- Pressing against Maddox's leg = Maddox is in danger he hasn't recognized yet
- Sitting calmly in otherwise tense space = the space is safer than it appears, trust his assessment

## The Phoenix Patch

The white chest marking becomes a minor plot thread. Older residents of Frederick react to it—some with recognition, some with unease. A historical photograph at the county archive shows a dog with a nearly identical marking beside one of the Ironveil founders in 1923.

Whether this is coincidence, lineage, or something the Ashborne recognizes and fears is left deliberately ambiguous throughout the game. But Dexter seems immune to the entity's direct influence in ways that matter. He can function in maximum-phenomenon zones where electronic equipment fails and humans experience overwhelming dread. He remains operational.

The implication is that there's something about Dexter—or something about the Phoenix marking—that the Ashborne either recognizes as hostile or cannot affect. Neither Maddox nor Dexter investigates this too closely. They just use the advantage it provides.

---

## Relationship with Maddox

The relationship is not owner-and-pet. It is partner-and-partner. Maddox would describe Dexter as "my dog" but that phrase understates the depth of mutual dependence. Maddox makes decisions based on Dexter's input. When Dexter is unsettled, Maddox is unsettled. When Dexter is calm, Maddox trusts the assessment.

By Act I, Maddox is increasingly relying on Dexter as the only truly trustworthy instrument in an investigation where every electronic system is suspect. By Act III, Dexter is the core of Maddox's sensory apparatus—his eyes, ears, and instinct combined.

Maddox talks to Dexter constantly. Full sentences, ongoing commentary, genuine dialogue. Dexter listens. His responses (ear position, body posture, direction of focus) answer back in a language Maddox has learned to read fluently.

## Specific Quirks & Behaviors

- Gets a puppuccino at the café on Market Street. The barista knows his name and Dexter's coffee order (small, extra whipped cream).

- Sleeps at the foot of Maddox's bed. Has strong opinions about being woken up before 7am, which Maddox respects without resentment.

- Dexter has watched Maddox use the slim jim set enough times that he sits and waits with the specific patience of a dog who knows exactly how long this will take.

- Has never bitten anyone who didn't have it coming. His judgement on who "has it coming" is more accurate than most human assessments.

- If Dexter doesn't like you, Maddox already knows. Conversely, if Dexter accepts you, he's giving you a kind of clearance that matters more than anything official.

- Can be directed with hand signals alone—useful in situations where voice communication would compromise cover.

- Tolerates equipment being attached to him (camera harness, sensor vest) because he understands it's necessary. Doesn't enjoy it, but accepts it.

- Maintains perfect heel position during fieldwork. Doesn't need a leash most of the time, but wears one for legal purposes at some locations.

- Has specific responses to "work" versus "off-duty." When the gear comes out, so does full focus. When Maddox says "break," Dexter relaxes immediately.

---

## Notes

- Dexter has never had food aggression or resource guarding issues. He shares space with other dogs when necessary (like Scout and Finn), though he prefers being Maddox's singular companion.

- He is not a guard dog in the traditional sense. He doesn't prevent people from approaching or entering spaces. He alerts, he assesses, and he only escalates if Maddox indicates threat response is warranted. His protection is selective and intelligent.

- He ages well. At five, he's still in prime condition, though he's starting to show the wisdom in his eyes that some people find unsettling. He watches too carefully. He knows too much.

- He has no interest in toys. Treats are functional, not entertainment. His satisfaction comes from work, from Maddox's approval, and from the simple presence of his person.

- He is not afraid. Not because he's oblivious, but because he trusts Maddox completely. Whatever comes, they'll face it together.
