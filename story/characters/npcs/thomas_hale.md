# Character Profile — Thomas Hale

---

## Basic Info

| | |
|---|---|
| **Full Name** | Thomas Michael Hale |
| **Age** | 51 (at time of death) |
| **Occupation** | Frederick City IT Director (deceased) |
| **Relationship** | Maddox's client; the inciting death |
| **Date of Death** | Saturday, April 12, 2025 |

---

## Appearance

Thomas Hale was a man of ordinary appearance — slightly overweight, gray-brown hair starting to thin, the kind of face you'd forget ten minutes after meeting him. He wore glasses for reading and often forgot to take them off. His work wardrobe was business casual — polo shirts, khaki pants, sensible shoes. He moved with the slightly stooped posture of someone who spent a lot of time at a desk.

---

## Personality

Thomas Hale was thorough, careful, and had been with the city for nineteen years. He was the kind of IT director who did his job competently without trying to be visible about it. He dismissed Maddox's initial findings about the anomalous partition — not because he was covering it up, but because he genuinely believed it was legacy junk data and didn't want the audit paperwork.

He was wrong.

He was not incompetent. He was cautious, experienced in navigating city bureaucracy, and genuinely believed that some technical problems could be safely ignored. He was not malicious. He was just tired and operating under the assumption that if something had been present for years without causing critical failures, it probably wasn't an active threat.

---

## The Hidden Documentation

Over fourteen months before his death, Hale noticed anomalies in the city network:
- Power draw spikes at specific times that didn't correlate with any known system
- Packet loss on segments with no obvious cause
- Firmware integrity checks failing on hardware that showed no signs of damage
- Permissions escalations that traced back to the anomalous partition but didn't originate from any known user account

He documented all of this in a TrueCrypt/VeraCrypt container on his personal workstation partition, separate from city systems. He labeled it cautiously ("weird_notes") and used a personal shorthand for his observations. He didn't report these findings because he didn't have language for them. He didn't understand what he was seeing, so reporting it would just create panic and paperwork without leading anywhere.

He was wrong about that too.

His documentation is one of the game's key resource threads. When Maddox cracks Hale's workstation and finds the encrypted container, Hale's fourteen months of observations become the timeline that contextualizes everything Maddox has found in the partition. Hale's notes describe the same phenomenon from a network administrator's perspective that Maddox is experiencing from the penetration tester's perspective. Together, they provide a complete picture.

---

## The Death

Official determination: Cardiac event. He was found in the server room at approximately 4:47 PM on Saturday, April 12, unresponsive. He died during transport to Frederick Memorial. He was 51 and had no prior cardiac history, which was unusual but not impossible.

The actual story: Hale was in the server room doing routine maintenance when the partition spiked with the same kind of activity that had characterized Maddox's investigation. Hale, being experienced, recognized this as significant. He made a decision to look directly at what was occurring. He initiated a direct system query on the anomalous partition.

This was the moment it noticed him.

Hale's cardiac event was real — he did have a heart attack. But it was triggered by electrical interference from the partition's manifestation as it became aware of his attention. His heart was perfectly healthy until the moment the Ashborne reached through the network and stopped it.

---

## What He Means to the Story

Hale is the death that begins everything. He's the reason Maddox is in the server room in the first place. He's the ghost that haunts the entire investigation — Maddox never met him alive, but investigating his death means coming to know him through his documentation, his careful notes, his fear.

Hale's documentation is also the bridge between the network-level data and the historical-level knowledge. Hale noticed when the phenomenon started (approximately 16 months before the game's start, coinciding with a city infrastructure upgrade that gave the partition stronger connectivity). This timing connects to the Ironveil's records — something about the 2024 infrastructure work destabilized the seal that had held for 160 years.

---

## Role in Investigation

Hale appears in the game only through his documentation and the investigation of his death. The player piece together who he was through:
- Interviews with colleagues (they describe him as competent, careful, quiet)
- His workstation setup (orderly, documented, professional)
- His hidden notes (revealing his fear and confusion)
- Photography of his physical workspace
- Detective Okafor's investigation (which overlaps with Maddox's)

Through these pieces, Hale becomes a character even though he's already dead at the game's start.

---

## Notes on His Character

- He was good at his job in a conventional sense but unprepared for the unconventional
- He was not corrupt or careless, but he was also not willing to create institutional chaos over anomalies he couldn't explain
- He was afraid — his notes show increasing anxiety as the anomalies accumulated and he couldn't find a rational explanation
- He was responsible (he documented everything rather than ignoring it completely) but not brave (he didn't report what he was finding)
- His death is tragic in the sense that it's the result of being in the wrong place with the right knowledge at the worst possible moment

Maddox never blames Hale. He understands by the end of Act I that Hale was trying to handle something incomprehensible with the tools available to an IT director. That's not negligence. That's just being human and unprepared.
