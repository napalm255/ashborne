# Character Profile — Nora Grey

---

## Basic Info

| | |
|---|---|
| **Full Name** | Nora Marie Grey |
| **Age** | 31 |
| **Occupation** | ER nurse, Frederick Memorial Hospital |
| **Relationship** | Maddox's younger sister |
| **Location** | Frederick, Maryland — lives in West Market Street neighborhood |

---

## Appearance

Nora shares Maddox's dark hair but wears hers longer, usually in a practical braid when she's at the hospital. She has his same blue eyes but they carry a different quality — where Maddox's look like they're running background processes, Nora's look like they're assessing immediate situations. She's slightly taller than Maddox, carrying herself with the straight-backed posture of someone who's spent years on her feet in high-pressure environments.

She wears scrubs outside the hospital more often than not (she's just come from a shift, doesn't bother changing). When she does dress up, she favors practical, no-nonsense clothing — good jeans, solid colors, minimal jewelry. A small silver cross on a chain (family piece, worn since their grandmother gave it to her at sixteen).

---

## Personality

Nora is the version of the Grey family that actually talks to people. She's sharp, pragmatic, and has the specific energy of someone who has seen genuine emergencies and therefore does not have patience for manufactured ones. She and Maddox have the comfortable friction of siblings who are genuinely close but rarely say so directly.

She's one of the few people who calls him Maddog without irony. She also calls him an idiot with genuine affection on a regular basis. "You're an idiot" from Nora means "I love you and you're scaring me" — Maddox understands this distinction perfectly.

She has a dark sense of humor — the kind that develops from working twelve-hour shifts in an ER, from losing patients you tried to save, from laughing because the alternative is crying. She appreciates when Maddox's dry wit surfaces; they share the same understanding that sometimes the only appropriate response to terrible situations is to be funny about it.

---

## Role in Story

### Act I

Nora appears at a critical story moment: Thomas Hale's death. She's the one who text Maddox that a city employee has come into the ER (she doesn't share patient details, but she knows Maddox was doing city work). She's the bridge between Maddox's investigation and the official story. She provides medical context for what killed Hale (officially cardiac event, which is technically true but obscures everything interesting). She's also the first person Maddox considers calling when he realizes something is wrong.

By Act I's end, Nora has noticed that Maddox looks worse than he normally does after a case. She asks him directly if he's in trouble. He deflects. She doesn't push because that's their dynamic — she asks once, he has the option to tell her, he usually doesn't. She accepts this.

### Act II & III

Nora becomes increasingly important as the personal stakes layer. Frederick Memorial is connected to the city network (medical records, patient administration, pharmacy systems). If the Ashborne spreads, Nora is literally in the middle of it. Maddox doesn't say this to her, but she figures it out. She also figures out that Maddox is in deeper than he's admitting. She starts cross-referencing Maddox's vague references to what she sees in hospital reports (unusual patient admissions, staff behavioral anomalies, power disruptions in specific areas).

In the final acts, Nora becomes a secondary information channel. She can't act directly in the investigation, but she can observe and report. This creates tension — she's worried about her brother, frustrated that he won't tell her what's happening, and determined to be useful anyway.

---

## Relationships to Other Characters

**Maddox**: Younger brother. She worries about him. She respects his expertise but thinks his tendency to investigate alone is going to get him killed someday. Wren, when she enters the picture, earns Nora's immediate approval because she's clearly capable and doesn't coddle Maddox. Nora sees her as a positive influence.

**Dexter**: Nora likes Dexter more than she likes most people. Dexter seems to understand her shifts — on nights when she's had a particularly bad shift, Dexter senses it and is less bouncy about greeting her. This matters more to her than she would admit.

**Family**: Her parents are alive but live in Hagerstown. She's close enough to see them monthly but far enough that she has her own life. She doesn't discuss the investigation with them; Maddox's work was always his own thing in the family framework.

## Skills & Professional Context

- ER nursing (shift work, trauma response, medication management)
- Triage assessment and rapid decision-making
- Patient communication (she's genuinely good at making scared people feel safer)
- Medical administration systems (Epic EHR — she knows how to navigate it and where the anomalies in the system would show up)
- Physical fitness (ER shifts require mobility and stamina)

## Notes

- She does a mix of day shifts and night shifts; her schedule rotates. This affects when Maddox can reach her.
- She has a running joke with Maddox about his go-bag always being packed: "Normal people have weekend plans. You have emergency plans."
- She once asked Maddox directly what he was so afraid of that he needed to be ready to leave at any moment. He didn't answer. She hasn't asked again.
- She knows Sal Mercer (they've crossed paths at the hospital a few times with injured contractors), and she has professional respect for him.
- If she figures out fully that Maddox is in supernatural-level danger, she will try to help despite having no skills for it. This will frustrate Maddox and impress him simultaneously.
