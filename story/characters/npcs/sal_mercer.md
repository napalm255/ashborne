## Appearance

Sal is stocky, built for strength and durability rather than anything else. He has dark hair kept short and practical (he's spent enough time under vehicles that long hair is a liability). His hands are perpetually stained with engine grease in the creases — he washes thoroughly but some things don't come clean. He has a scar across the back of his left hand from a car door incident eight years ago. He wears work boots, jeans, and shop t-shirts with faded logos of motorcycle manufacturers and local businesses. A simple silver ring on his left ring finger — he's married, though his wife lives in Baltimore and they maintain a quiet long-distance arrangement out of mutual agreement and professional necessity.

He moves with the economical precision of someone who knows his body's limitations and works within them efficiently.

---

## Personality

Sal has known Maddox long enough to not be surprised by anything and to still be occasionally surprised anyway. He runs a well-regarded independent shop on the south end of downtown, specializes in motorcycles and older vehicles, and is the kind of person who can fabricate a part from scratch and doesn't see why that's impressive.

He calls Maddox "Maddog" the way most people say a person's name. He's the only person whose texts Maddox answers at 2am without irritation.

His shop becomes a de facto base of operations during the investigation. He doesn't ask too many questions, which is a quality Maddox values deeply. He does ask *some* questions, which is how Maddox doesn't spiral completely into isolation and paranoia.

He has a dry, practical sense of humor. He won't make jokes; he'll just deadpan an observation that's funny because it's accurate. When Maddox shows up at the shop at 3am looking wrecked, Sal doesn't ask. He puts coffee on. When Maddox asks if he can store something in the back room, Sal says "That depends on whether it's going to try to kill me" (approximately 60% joking).

---

## Background & Military Service

The photograph in his locked lockbox (hidden in the parts room) is a military uniform — Army National Guard, Maryland unit, circa 2003–2005 deployment era. He did a tour. He doesn't talk about it. It explains some things about his composure under pressure: Sal has seen genuinely bad situations and responded appropriately. A situation involving anomalies and potential danger doesn't rattle him the way it might rattle someone without that background.

He never corrects people who don't know, but if asked directly, he will confirm it matter-of-factly and then change the subject. The experience is part of his personality — it's why he doesn't panic, why he can assess danger accurately, why he has a legal handgun in his lockbox and knows how to use it. It's not a defining trait for him, but it's load-bearing.

---

## Business & Operations

Sal's shop serves Frederick's motorcycle and vintage car community. He does custom builds, repairs, restoration work. He has a Bambu Lab X1C 3D printer in the back office that he uses to fabricate obsolete automotive brackets for restoration projects — lower resolution than Maddox's, but functional for production parts.

His inventory system is a spreadsheet he's been grudgingly updating since 2019. He does parts reorder manually via phone calls to distributors he's known for years. Maddox has quietly automated his parts reorder system anyway by creating a database that pulls from the spreadsheet and generates order summaries. Sal pretended not to notice for six months, then started using it. He's never directly acknowledged it but he's clearly grateful.

## Role in Story

### Act I

Sal is Maddox's fallback, his emotional anchor to normalcy. After the Hale death and before Wren, Sal is the person Maddox talks to (in his limited way). Sal provides practical support — the use of the shop for gear storage, a place to work late without being visible from Maddox's apartment, occasional "that's a stupid idea" feedback when Maddox is planning something particularly reckless.

The second visit to Mercer's (INT-04, Room 4B) is where Dexter finds Sal's lockbox in the parts room. Maddox has the option to open it without Sal knowing (which loses 1 rapport and makes Sal suspicious) or to tell Sal what Dexter found (which gains rapport and gets him access to information about Sal's background). The better play is transparency.

### Act II & III

Sal becomes increasingly involved as logistics support. He has tools Maddox needs for specific device builds. He can repair damaged equipment fast. He becomes a secondary safe location when Maddox's apartment starts being problematic. He also becomes concerned — he's watching his oldest friend get deeper into something that doesn't have a clean resolution. He can't help directly (he's not equipped for what's happening), but he can make sure Maddox has what he needs to try.

By Act III, Sal has figured out that something is genuinely wrong at a level beyond normal danger. He doesn't know it's literally supernatural, but he knows Maddox is scared in a way Maddox hasn't been scared in the entire decade he's known him. This matters to Sal.

## Relationships to Other Characters

**Maddox**: Best friend, primary support system. He's invested in Maddox's well-being. He worries.

**Dexter**: Sal and Dexter understand each other with the quiet acceptance of two creatures who operate efficiently without unnecessary display. Dexter is completely relaxed in Sal's shop — one of the few places where that's true.

**Nora**: Professional acquaintance. They've crossed paths at the hospital (Nora has treated injured contractors from Sal's shop, dealt with vehicle accident victims). Mutual professional respect.

**Vivienne Colt**: Sal has sold her salvaged parts before (she has a restoration hobby alongside the antiquities business). They're cordial. She approves of him as Maddox's friend.

The cash and gun become available as resources if Act III goes badly.

## Dialogue Voice

Sal speaks in short, practical sentences. He uses the minimum words necessary to convey information. He doesn't repeat himself. He listens more than he talks. When he gives advice, it's direct and considered — he's thought about it before he says it. His humor is observational and lands quietly.

Example: If Maddox shows up with gear he clearly stole from somewhere, Sal will look at it for a moment and then say: "That's a felony." If Maddox says it's necessary, Sal will nod and ask what he needs to make it work. He's not judging; he's assessing.

## Notes

- He's kept the same shop location for 9 years. He owns the building; he's not going anywhere.
- He has a specific coffee order (dark roast, no sugar, black) and an espresso machine in the office that Maddox has learned to use because Sal's coffee tastes better than his own.
- He has a wife (Maria) who lives in Baltimore and works as a nurse. They see each other on weekends. It's a stable, quiet relationship. Maddox has met her once and approved.
- He has never asked Maddox why the go-bag is always packed. But the fact that it is packed doesn't surprise him.
- In Act III, if things get truly dangerous, Sal will try to help with the only leverage he has — physical strength and willingness to act. Maddox will spend a significant portion of Act III trying to keep Sal out of it. Sal will ignore him.
