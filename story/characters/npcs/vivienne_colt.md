## Appearance

Vivienne is a woman who has earned her steel-gray hair and the lines around her eyes through years of careful observation and judicious decision-making. She dresses elegantly without ostentation — high-quality fabrics in neutral tones, a single piece of jewelry (rotating: a silver brooch, a brass compass, an old cameo pin). Her hands are those of someone who has handled countless objects — slight tremor from arthritis in the fingers, but steady. She wears reading glasses on a beaded chain.

She moves slowly, not from incapacity but from deliberation. Every motion is considered. She doesn't hurry. She doesn't waste energy.

---

## Personality

Vivienne runs a cluttered, wonderful antique shop that smells like old paper and cedar and is generally the kind of place tourists photograph the outside of without going in. She knows the provenance of every object in the shop and the history of half the buildings in Frederick. She also knows things she's not supposed to know, and has the careful manner of someone who has spent decades deciding who is worth telling.

She's been waiting for someone to find the Ashborne and come to her. She didn't expect it to be a guy in a jacket with too many pockets who had a pitbull tied up outside.

Her speech is precise, occasionally Victorian in phrasing (she's read extensively in 19th-century history and some of it has soaked into her natural voice). She doesn't waste words, but the words she uses are chosen with care. When she's being deliberately vague, it's calculated vagueness — a test to see if you're paying attention.

She has a dry sense of humor that surfaces occasionally and lands like a punch because you weren't expecting it.

---

## Background & Knowledge

Vivienne is the unofficial historian of Frederick. She's not formally credentialed (she has an MFA in Art History from Johns Hopkins, nothing more specific), but through decades of collecting, research, and careful attention to city development, she's become the go-to source for what was where and why it matters.

She's been waiting for someone to find the Ashborne because she's known about it for approximately fifteen years. She doesn't know how it got into the network infrastructure. She knows it's been in Frederick since 1864 and that a group of people — the Ironveil — sealed it. She knows the sealing was designed to hold indefinitely. She also knows that seals weaken, and she's been noticing subtle signs for years.

Her knowledge is historical and intuitive rather than technical. She doesn't understand network partitions or RF signals. But she understands patterns, and she's seen the pattern repeat across 160 years: the Ashborne's presence correlates with specific types of death, specific emotional states in affected areas, specific behaviors in people exposed to it.

She's not been actively researching this (she's learned patience). She's been cataloging and maintaining records, waiting for someone who could bridge her historical knowledge and the technical understanding the situation actually requires.

## Role in Story

### Act I

Vivienne's first appearance is critical. Maddox walks into her shop to ask about an old signal equipment piece. He stays to show her the cipher pattern from the partition. She recognizes it immediately and takes him to the archive. She shows him the Confederate Army field document from August 1864 with the same symbolic structure. She provides the historical context that prevents Maddox from dismissing what he found as a network error.

She becomes the first named connection to the Ironveil. She doesn't tell him everything she knows (strategic information rationing), but she tells him enough to reframe his investigation from "technical problem" to "161-year-old supernatural seal failure."

She also meets Dexter in this scene. She offers her hand. Dexter approaches carefully and sniffs it, then leans into a scratch behind his ear. Maddox watches this and visibly relaxes. "He doesn't like most people," Maddox observes. Vivienne smiles. "Then I'm honored."

### Act II

Vivienne becomes more actively engaged. She provides artifacts (the brass compass for the T3-02 Ashborne Compass), documents from her archive, and introductions to other people in Frederick who carry Ironveil knowledge (though she doesn't present it as Ironveil knowledge — just as "contacts who know Frederick's history").

She also becomes Maddox's ethical compass on certain questions. When the sealing ritual becomes real and immediate, Vivienne anchors him to the principle that the Ashborne must be contained, not destroyed. Some things cannot be destroyed. They must be held. This distinction matters.

### Act III

Vivienne remains a resource and counsel, but Maddox has moved into the active phase. She provides final contextual pieces but the action belongs to him now.

## The Shop (Gameplay Relevant)

**INT-03 Significance**:
- Primary historical resource hub
- Artifact source (certain items can only be acquired from Vivienne, through rapport or story progression)
- Safe location (Vivienne's workspace provides quiet space to think and document)
- NPC dialogue and information delivery location

**The Archive**:
Contains the bulk of Ironveil research documents, period maps, and contextual materials. Access is gated by Vivienne's trust level and story phase. She won't just hand over sensitive documents to someone she doesn't trust. But as trust builds, she opens more. By Act II, you have archive access.

**The Sealed Envelope** (in private archive):
A significant story object that Vivienne has never opened. She knows what it is (Ironveil internal documents, personal letters possibly). She's chosen not to open it. This choice is a character beat — what does it mean that she's had access to crucial information and decided not to look? When you interact with it, you have the option to ask her permission or take it anyway. The "right" choice is asking. This gains significant rapport.

## Relationships to Other Characters

**Maddox**: She's been waiting for him, though she wouldn't phrase it that way. She approves of his approach — technical, curious, willing to acknowledge evidence over framework. She becomes his ally and advisor.

**Wren Calloway**: Professional relationship from before the game starts. Wren has brought building survey photographs asking for Vivienne's identification of architectural elements in historic buildings. They're cordial, share similar methodologies (evidence-based, careful, thorough). Vivienne will vouch for Wren if Maddox has doubts.

**Dexter**: She and Dexter form a quiet mutual respect. He's protective of Maddox. She understands this. She doesn't try to befriend him; she respects his role.

---

## Dialogue Voice

Vivienne speaks carefully and precisely. She uses more formal vocabulary than most contemporary people, but this isn't affectation — it's just how she naturally speaks after decades of studying 19th-century documents. Her sentences are complete and grammatically proper. She pauses before answering questions, as if consulting something internal. She doesn't rush. She's comfortable with silence.

When she's withholding information deliberately, she's courteous about it. She'll say something like "That's a story for later" or "When you understand more, you'll be ready for that part."

## Notes

- Her Instagram account (@coltandcompass) was active for four years and had good engagement. It's been dormant for three weeks as of the game's start. This is unusual for her cadence and might be a signal to a player who researches her.

- She has a cat (orange tabby, unfriendly) that lives in the shop. The cat dislikes most people. Dexter the cat maintains a careful territorial respect with Dexter the dog.

- She's been running the shop for 31 years. She's not going anywhere.

- She has a small circle of friends in Frederick's historic preservation community. She's not reclusive, just selective. She hosts dinners occasionally for this circle.

- Her relationship to the Ironveil is not familial (she's not descended from the original group). She discovered them through research and decided to maintain their work. This is a choice she made, not a birthright.

- She will never willingly lie to Maddox. She'll withhold information, but she won't deceive. This becomes a foundation of trust between them.
